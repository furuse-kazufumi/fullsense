I turned a solo project's entire development history into a 63-article, 4-language knowledge base.

While building FullSense — an AI ecosystem that runs on a home PC (llmesh / llive / llove) — I wrote up design decisions, benchmarks, and failures almost daily. Past 60 articles, I consolidated everything into a knowledge base ("FullSense KB") on Qiita Team.

It reads less like documentation and more like the story of the project:

- **A story-based reading guide** — prologue to act 10, the dev history is the table of contents
- **Every article in 4 languages** (JA/EN/ZH/KO) — 37 articles localized in 28 minutes by 37 parallel AI agents
- **Plain-language editions** — the hard parts retold with goldfish-pond and school-exam metaphors
- **4-panel manga + a lion-dance mascot** — born from a Japanese pun connecting "chewing things up for you" with "divine possession"
- **Honest disclosure throughout** — suspicious wins get audited, failures stay in the record

Biggest lesson: documentation compounds when you co-write it with AI. The articles serve human readers and double as context for the AI agents themselves — they now make proposals grounded in past decisions.

The KB itself is team-internal, but the entrances are public:

- Public articles (39, mostly Japanese): https://qiita.com/furuse-kazufumi
- The code (Apache-2.0): https://github.com/furuse-kazufumi/fullsense
